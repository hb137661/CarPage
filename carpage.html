Challenger

The Challenger is one big bruiser, with heft better suited to cruising or drag-racing at the strip than turning laps on a racetrack. Powertrains include the standard 305-hp 3.6-liter V-6 and an eight-speed automatic; one 5.7-liter Hemi V-8 makes 375 hp with the standard six-speed manual, and one (with fuel-saving tech) makes 372 hp with the optional eight-speed auto. The Scat Pack option features a 485-hp 6.4-liter Hemi V-8; a six-speed manual is standard and an eight-speed auto is optional. Click Here

Charger

Ringbrothers stole the spotlight with its Hellcat-powered AMC Javelin, but it also debuted another Mopar machine at the 2017 SEMA show and this one is just as mean looking. Signifying the Spring Green, Wisconsin company's first-ever Mopar build is a 1969 Dodge Charger dubbed the Defector. The custom shop arguably didn't pour as much attention into the 1969 Charger as the Javelin build, but it's still a stunner. The custom Mopar took over 4,700 hours to complete and features some significant body and wheelbase changes, despite Ringbrothers' goal being to keep the Charger's iconic factory design intact. Chevelle

This 1969 Chevrolet Chevelle SS396 is powered by a 402ci V8 from a later car paired to close-ratio Muncie 4-speed manual and Posi-traction rear axle. The seller is the fourth owner, and the previous owner added 4k miles from 2001 to late 2016. A copy of the GM build sheet (see gallery) identifies the Factory options including the high output L78 396ci (375 hp) V8, 4-speed M22 heavy duty close-ratio transmission, 12-bolt Posi-traction rear end with a 4:10 ratio, tachometer, power steering, and power brakes. The previous owner had the engine and transmission rebuilt in 2001 with an Edelbrock intake, headers, and a mild cam. The interior was partially refurbished in 2016, and the car has received new tires. Replacement of two wheel cylinders and an oil change were performed during the seller’s 500 miles of driving. This Chevelle is sold with service records dating to 1997 and a clean Ontario title. Click Here
